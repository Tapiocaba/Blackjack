Introduction

Although basic strategy is not enough to overcome the house edge and guarantee that a player profits from Blackjack, following the rules of basic strategy is the first step in learning how to play.

At the beginning of the game, both the player and dealer is dealt 2 cards. Whether the cards dealt contain an Ace determines whether it is a Soft or Hard hand. Depending on which type of hand you have, your strategy should change. Both have different odds on winning against the dealer.

Soft Totals

A soft total is any hand in which one of the first two cards is an ace. The aceâ€™s value can either be a 1 or an 11, depending on the situation. Typically, soft hands imply more liquity and better options for the player. When you have a soft hand, it can lead to some of the best chances to win!

Strategy:

  • If you have a soft 20 and 19, then stand.
  • If you have a soft 18, hit. If against a visible 9 through Ace, otherwise stand.
  • If you have a soft 17 - 13, then hit.

Hard Totals

In a hard total hand, you won't have as many options to choose what to do. Here, you should always assume that the dealer has a 10 in the hole.

  • 17 and up always stands
  • Otherwise, you should always hit
