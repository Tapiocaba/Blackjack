Objective

Although basic strategy is not enough to overcome the house edge and guarantee that a player profits from Blackjack, following the rules of basic strategy is the first step in beating blackjack.

Dealing

The game is played with a standard 52-card pack. The dealer deals one card face up to each player, then one face up themselves. They then deal another round face up, but keep their card face down. In the end, each player will have two face-up cards, and the dealer will have one face-up card and one face-down.

The Game

Each player wants to beat the dealer by getting a hand count closer than the dealer to 21 without going over 21 or “busting”.

Playing the Game

The player to the left goes first, and the players proceed in clockwise direction. Each player must decide whether to “stand”, or not ask for another card, or “hit”, and request another card to get closer to 21. If a player decides to hit, they can continue hitting one card at a time until they either decide to stand or their hand coun

Number cards have their statue value, and face cards have a value of 10. An ace can either have a value of 1 or 11 depending on the rest of the cards in the player’s hand. If the player has an ace with a card other than a 10-card, it is known as a “soft hand” because the player can choose to count the ace as a 1 or 11 and move accordingly. If counting the ace as an 11 would result in the hand count exceeding 21 but counting it as a 1 would not, the ace would count

After the dealer has served all players, they turn their face-down card up. If the hand count is 17 or higher, the dealer must stand, otherwise they must draw cards one by one until the hand count is 17 or more. If the dealer has an ace and counting it as an 11 would make the hand count 17 or more, the dealer must count it as an 11.
